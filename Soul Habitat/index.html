The Vision

We have never been more connected.
And never more alone.

Two in five adults now live with deep, lasting loneliness. So many, in fact, that governments have begun appointing ministers to deal with it. The research leaves little room for doubt. Weak social connection shortens a life about as much as smoking fifteen cigarettes a day.

Soulhabitat begins with a quieter, more hopeful truth, one drawn from eighty years of Harvard research and a lifetime of building in remote places. The way we design our homes shapes the way we feel inside them.

Privacy without isolation

A home that gives you real quiet behind a closed door, with warm company a short walk away the moment you want it.

Connection by design

A shared meal, working side by side, a conversation by the pool. These are small everyday moments, and they only happen when the buildings around you make room for them.

Rooted in nature

Homes that feel like part of the orchard around them. No cars, no fences, just light and trees and the space to breathe.

A Day in the Life

What does living here
actually feel like?

From the first cup of coffee to the last ember of the fire pit, the rhythm is yours to set. Here is one ordinary, extraordinary day.

Morning

You wake in your own home and make coffee in your own kitchen. The door to the deck swings open into the trees, and for a while the day belongs entirely to you.

Late morning

Ninety seconds on foot brings you to the common house. Two neighbours are already working there. You pull up a chair beside them, or you take a quiet corner of your own. It's your call, every time.

Afternoon

A swim in the natural pool as the heat softens. Later, someone lights the fire pit. You wander over, or you don't, and either way nobody minds. That ease is the whole point.

Evening

Dinner comes together in the common kitchen, five of you cooking, laughter spilling across the terrace. Or your door is closed, a book is open, and the evening is quietly, completely your own.

When you're away

Lock the door and leave for a week, a month, a season. The staff tend the gardens and the pool, watch over your home, and have it ready and waiting the day you return. Belonging here asks nothing of you when you're gone.

The Place

Four and a half acres of living orchard.

Soulhabitat One sits on 7,460 square metres of central Koh Phangan, ten minutes from Thong Sala, Sri Thanu and Chaloklum, yet wrapped in the quiet of a mature tropical orchard. The land is owned in common, with no internal fences, no subdivisions and no roads cutting through the trees.

Durian, mangosteen, mango, rambutan and coconut grow where they have always grown. A freshwater stream runs year-round. Every vehicle stays at the entrance, so the estate itself belongs to footpaths, birdsong and the people who live here.

The Site

Designed so that paths cross naturally.

The homes are placed with intention. They sit close enough that a walk to the common house brings you past a neighbour, and far enough that every window frames trees rather than walls. The common building and natural pool sit at the heart, with the orchard breathing all around.

It is the geometry of a real community: privacy and proximity held in careful balance.

The Community & Governance

A community that holds together.

Good neighbours aren't left to chance. Soulhabitat is governed gently but clearly, with light structures that let trust grow and protect everyone's peace.

People who self-select

People drawn to community living tend to be warm and collaborative by nature. The right people find their way here, and that is the quiet engine behind everything that works.

One home, one voice

A fair one-home-one-vote system. Major decisions need a genuine majority, so no single person ever holds undue control over the place you all call home.

The Harmony Code

A short, reasonable set of shared commitments. It protects quiet hours, cares for shared spaces and keeps the estate's low-density calm. Minimal by design, and changed only by a vote.

Conflict, handled with grace

Disagreements are met openly: a friendly conversation first, gentle mediation at the monthly meeting if needed, and a clear, fair process beyond that to protect the community for the long term.

A Word from the Founder

Craig Henderson

I was born and raised on my family's tea estate in the Seychelles, educated in Scotland, and then spent decades building things in the difficult, remote corners of Southeast Asia. Pearl farms in Indonesia, Papua New Guinea, the Philippines, Myanmar and Thailand. Beyond teaching me how to build well in hard places, all of that gave me an obsession with doing things properly the first time. No shortcuts. No compromises on materials or craftsmanship.

Let me be completely honest about what Soulhabitat is. It's the home I'm building for myself. I will live here. I will be your neighbour. When you invest in this, you're trusting me with something important, and I take that seriously. Every decision has been made by someone who will live with its consequences.
